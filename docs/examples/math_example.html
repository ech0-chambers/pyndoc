Sample Assignment

Question 1

You are given a sample of metal and asked to determine its specific heat. You weigh the sample and find that it has a mass of \(9\ \mathrm{kg}\). You carefully add \(1.5226 \times 10^{3}\ \mathrm{J}\) of heat energy to the sample and find that its temperature rises by \(21\ \mathrm{K}\). What is the specific heat of the metal?

Solution

The specific heat capacity is the energy required to raise the temperature of a unit mass of a substance by one degree:

\[\begin{split} C & = \frac{E}{m \Delta T}\\ & = \frac{1.5226 \times 10^{3}\ \mathrm{J}}{9\ \mathrm{kg} \times 21\ \mathrm{K}}\\ & = 8.056\ \mathrm{J}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}\,\mathrm{kg}^{-1} \end{split}\]

So, the specific heat capacity is \(C = 8.056\ \mathrm{J}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}\,\mathrm{kg}^{-1}\).

Question 2

In an effort to stay awake for an all-night study session, a student makes a cup of coffee by first heating \(0.15\ \mathrm{kg}\) of water in a \(450\ \mathrm{W}\) kettle. How much heat must be added to the water to raise its temperature from \(27\ \mathrm{^{\circ}C}\) to \(90\ \mathrm{^{\circ}C}\)?

Solution

The specific heat capacity of water is \(C = 4180\ \mathrm{J}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}\,\mathrm{kg}^{-1}\). The energy required to raise the temperature of a substance is given by:

\[\begin{split} E & = m C \Delta T\\ & = 0.15\ \mathrm{kg} \times 4180\ \mathrm{J}\,\mathrm{K}^{-1}\,\mathrm{kg}^{-1} \times 63\ \mathrm{K}\\ & = 3.95 \times 10^{4}\ \mathrm{J} \end{split}\]

So, the total amount of energy needed is \(3.95 \times 10^{4}\ \mathrm{J}\), or \(39.5\ \mathrm{kJ}\).

The power is given as \(450\ \mathrm{W}\). The time required to heat the water is given by:

\[\begin{split} t & = \frac{E}{P}\\ & = \frac{3.95 \times 10^{4}\ \mathrm{J}}{450\ \mathrm{W}}\\ & = 87.78\ \mathrm{J}\,\mathrm{W}^{-1} \end{split}\]

Or, \(1\ \mathrm{min}\) and \(28\ \mathrm{s}\).
